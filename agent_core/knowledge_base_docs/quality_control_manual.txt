QUALITY CONTROL MANUAL
======================

1. QUALITY MANAGEMENT SYSTEM OVERVIEW

1.1 Purpose
This manual defines the quality control standards, inspection procedures, and
compliance requirements for all products and suppliers in the supply chain.
The quality management system is aligned with ISO-9001:2015 standards.

1.2 Scope
Quality control applies to all incoming materials, in-process production,
finished goods, and supplier-provided components across all product categories:
mechanical parts, electronic components, raw materials, packaging, and chemicals.

1.3 Quality Policy
We are committed to delivering products that meet or exceed customer expectations
through continuous improvement, defect prevention, and compliance with all
applicable regulatory standards.

2. INSPECTION PROCEDURES

2.1 Incoming Material Inspection
All incoming shipments undergo inspection within 24 hours of receipt:
- Visual inspection for damage and packaging integrity
- Quantity verification against purchase order
- Dimensional checks using calibrated instruments
- Material composition verification (for critical components)
- Documentation review (certificates of conformance, test reports)

2.2 Sampling Plans
- Standard lots (< 1000 units): AQL 1.0, General Inspection Level II
- Large lots (1000-10000 units): AQL 0.65, General Inspection Level II
- Critical components: 100% inspection regardless of lot size
- Reduced inspection: Available for Tier 1 suppliers with 10+ consecutive passed lots

2.3 Inspection Results Classification
- Pass: All measured parameters within specification limits
- Conditional Pass: Minor deviations that do not affect functionality (requires deviation approval)
- Fail: One or more critical parameters outside specification limits
- Hold: Pending additional testing or supplier clarification

2.4 Batch Identification
Each inspection is tracked by batch ID in the format BATCH-YYYY-NNN where YYYY
is the year and NNN is a sequential number. Batch IDs are linked to product IDs
for full traceability.

3. PRODUCT QUALITY STANDARDS

3.1 Mechanical Parts
- Dimensional accuracy: +/- 0.01mm for precision components
- Surface roughness: Ra <= 0.8 micrometers
- Hardness: Per material specification (HRC/HRB scale)
- Tensile strength: Per material specification
- Fatigue life: Minimum 10^6 cycles at rated load

3.2 Electronic Components
- Electrical parameters: Within datasheet specifications
- Solder joint quality: IPC-A-610 Class 2 minimum
- ESD sensitivity: Proper handling and packaging verified
- Operating temperature range: Per component specification
- Moisture sensitivity level: Proper dry-pack verified

3.3 Raw Materials
- Chemical composition: Within specified tolerances
- Physical properties: Density, viscosity, melting point as specified
- Purity level: Minimum 99.5% for industrial grade
- Shelf life: Minimum 80% remaining at time of receipt
- Storage conditions: Temperature and humidity per material SDS

3.4 Packaging Materials
- Structural integrity: Drop test from 1.2m height
- Moisture resistance: 24-hour humidity exposure test
- Print quality: Color accuracy within Delta E <= 3.0
- Dimensional accuracy: +/- 1mm
- Recyclability: Minimum 80% recyclable content

4. COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK

4.1 Regulatory Compliance
All products and suppliers must comply with:
- ISO-9001:2015 Quality Management Systems
- ISO-14001:2015 Environmental Management Systems (where applicable)
- RoHS Directive (Restriction of Hazardous Substances)
- REACH Regulation (Registration, Evaluation, Authorization of Chemicals)
- Country-specific import/export regulations

4.2 Supplier Compliance Requirements
- Annual self-assessment questionnaire
- Biennial on-site audit for Tier 1 and Tier 2 suppliers
- Immediate notification of any regulatory changes affecting products
- Maintenance of quality records for minimum 7 years
- Participation in corrective action processes when required

4.3 Compliance Status Categories
- Compliant: All requirements met, certifications current
- Conditionally Compliant: Minor gaps identified, remediation plan in place
- Non-Compliant: Critical gaps requiring immediate corrective action
- Suspended: Compliance status revoked pending investigation

4.4 Audit Schedule
- Internal audits: Quarterly
- Supplier audits: Per tier schedule (see Supplier Manual Section 5.2)
- Certification audits: Annual (ISO-9001, ISO-14001)
- Regulatory audits: As required by governing bodies

5. NON-CONFORMANCE MANAGEMENT

5.1 Non-Conformance Report (NCR) Process
Step 1: Inspector identifies non-conformance and creates NCR
Step 2: Quality engineer classifies severity (Critical/Major/Minor)
Step 3: Affected material is quarantined and labeled
Step 4: Root cause analysis conducted within 5 business days
Step 5: Corrective action implemented and verified
Step 6: NCR closed with documentation of resolution

5.2 Severity Classification
- Critical: Safety risk or complete loss of function. Requires immediate containment
  and customer notification within 24 hours.
- Major: Significant performance degradation or appearance defect. Requires
  corrective action within 10 business days.
- Minor: Cosmetic or documentation issue with no functional impact. Requires
  corrective action within 30 business days.

5.3 Disposition Options
- Return to Supplier: For supplier-caused defects
- Rework: If cost-effective and quality can be assured
- Use As-Is: With engineering deviation approval (minor issues only)
- Scrap: When rework is not feasible or cost-effective

6. CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT

6.1 Quality Metrics Dashboard
Key metrics tracked monthly:
- First Pass Yield (target: >= 98%)
- Cost of Quality (target: <= 2% of revenue)
- Customer complaint rate (target: <= 0.1%)
- Supplier defect rate (target: <= 500 DPMO)
- Corrective action closure rate (target: >= 90% on time)

6.2 Improvement Initiatives
- Kaizen events: Monthly focused improvement workshops
- Six Sigma projects: For systemic quality issues
- Supplier development programs: For underperforming suppliers
- Training programs: Quarterly quality awareness sessions

7. DOCUMENT CONTROL

7.1 Document Hierarchy
- Level 1: Quality Policy (approved by executive management)
- Level 2: Quality Manual (this document, approved by Quality Director)
- Level 3: Procedures and Work Instructions (approved by department managers)
- Level 4: Forms, checklists, and records (controlled by Quality team)

7.2 Revision Control
All quality documents follow a formal revision control process. Changes require
review and approval by the document owner and Quality Director. Superseded
versions are archived for a minimum of 7 years.
