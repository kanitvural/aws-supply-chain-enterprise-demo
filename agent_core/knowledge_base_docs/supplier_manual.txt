SUPPLIER MANAGEMENT MANUAL
===========================

1. SUPPLIER ONBOARDING PROCESS

1.1 Registration Requirements
All new suppliers must complete the Supplier Registration Form and provide:
- Valid business license and tax identification
- Proof of insurance (minimum $1M general liability)
- Quality certifications (ISO-9001 required, ISO-14001 preferred)
- Bank account details for payment processing
- Emergency contact information

1.2 Evaluation Criteria
Suppliers are evaluated on a 5-point scale across the following dimensions:
- Product quality and consistency (weight: 30%)
- Delivery reliability and lead times (weight: 25%)
- Pricing competitiveness (weight: 20%)
- Communication responsiveness (weight: 15%)
- Sustainability and compliance record (weight: 10%)

1.3 Approval Workflow
Step 1: Procurement team reviews registration documents
Step 2: Quality team conducts initial audit (on-site or remote)
Step 3: Finance team verifies banking and credit references
Step 4: Compliance team checks regulatory standing
Step 5: Procurement manager grants final approval

2. SUPPLIER CLASSIFICATION AND TIERS

2.1 Tier Definitions
- Tier 1 (Strategic): Rating >= 4.5, annual volume > $500K, long-term contracts
- Tier 2 (Preferred): Rating >= 3.5, annual volume > $100K, standard terms
- Tier 3 (Approved): Rating >= 2.5, annual volume > $10K, probationary terms
- Tier 4 (Probationary): New suppliers or those under performance review

2.2 Tier Benefits
Tier 1 suppliers receive priority order allocation, extended payment terms (Net 60),
and access to joint development programs. Tier 2 suppliers receive standard payment
terms (Net 30) and quarterly business reviews. Tier 3 and 4 suppliers operate under
Net 15 payment terms with monthly performance monitoring.

3. DISCOUNT CODE POLICY

3.1 Discount Code Format
All supplier discount codes follow the pattern DISC-XXX-NNNN where XXX is a
three-letter supplier abbreviation and NNNN is a four-digit numeric identifier.
Example: DISC-ACM-1234 for Acme Industrial Supply.

3.2 Discount Application Rules
- Discount codes are confidential and must not be shared externally
- Each discount code is tied to a specific supplier agreement
- Discounts apply only to orders exceeding the minimum order quantity
- Discount codes expire at the end of the contract period
- Maximum discount cannot exceed 25% of list price

3.3 Discount Approval Authority
- Discounts up to 10%: Procurement specialist approval
- Discounts 10-20%: Procurement manager approval
- Discounts 20-25%: VP of Supply Chain approval

4. ORDER MANAGEMENT

4.1 Purchase Order Process
All purchase orders must include: supplier ID, product specifications, quantity,
agreed unit price, delivery date, shipping terms, and applicable discount code.
POs above $50,000 require dual approval from procurement and finance.

4.2 Lead Time Standards
- Standard orders: 14-21 business days
- Rush orders: 5-7 business days (15% surcharge applies)
- Emergency orders: 1-3 business days (30% surcharge applies)

4.3 Order Modification Policy
Orders may be modified up to 48 hours before the scheduled production start date.
Modifications after this window may incur change fees of up to 5% of order value.
Cancellations within 24 hours of production start incur a 15% cancellation fee.

5. SUPPLIER PERFORMANCE MONITORING

5.1 Key Performance Indicators
- On-time delivery rate (target: >= 95%)
- Quality acceptance rate (target: >= 98%)
- Order accuracy rate (target: >= 99%)
- Response time to inquiries (target: <= 24 hours)
- Defect rate per million units (target: <= 500 DPMO)

5.2 Performance Review Schedule
- Tier 1: Annual comprehensive review with quarterly check-ins
- Tier 2: Semi-annual review with monthly KPI reporting
- Tier 3: Quarterly review with weekly KPI reporting
- Tier 4: Monthly review with daily KPI reporting

5.3 Corrective Action Process
If a supplier falls below performance thresholds:
Step 1: Issue a Supplier Corrective Action Request (SCAR)
Step 2: Supplier submits root cause analysis within 5 business days
Step 3: Supplier implements corrective actions within 30 days
Step 4: Verification audit conducted within 60 days
Step 5: If unresolved, supplier is downgraded one tier

6. COMMUNICATION PROTOCOLS

6.1 Primary Contact Requirements
Each supplier must designate a primary account manager and a backup contact.
Contact information must be updated within 5 business days of any personnel change.

6.2 Escalation Path
Level 1: Supplier account manager (response within 4 hours)
Level 2: Supplier regional director (response within 8 hours)
Level 3: Supplier VP of operations (response within 24 hours)

6.3 Dispute Resolution
All disputes are first addressed through direct negotiation between procurement
and the supplier account manager. If unresolved within 10 business days, the
matter escalates to a joint review committee. Formal mediation is the final step
before contract termination proceedings.

7. CONTRACT MANAGEMENT

7.1 Standard Contract Terms
- Initial contract period: 12 months
- Renewal: Automatic annual renewal unless 90-day notice given
- Payment terms: Based on supplier tier (see Section 2.2)
- Liability cap: 100% of annual contract value
- Force majeure: Standard clause covering natural disasters, pandemics, and government actions

7.2 Contract Termination
Grounds for immediate termination:
- Fraud or misrepresentation
- Repeated quality failures (3+ consecutive failed inspections)
- Regulatory non-compliance
- Breach of confidentiality
- Insolvency or bankruptcy filing
