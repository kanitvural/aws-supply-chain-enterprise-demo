INVENTORY MANAGEMENT PROCEDURES
================================

1. INVENTORY CONTROL OVERVIEW

1.1 Purpose
This document defines the procedures for managing inventory across all warehouse
locations, including receiving, storage, cycle counting, replenishment, and
disposition of supply chain products.

1.2 Scope
These procedures apply to all product categories maintained in the inventory
system: mechanical parts, electronic components, raw materials, packaging
materials, and chemicals.

1.3 Inventory System
All inventory transactions are recorded in the centralized inventory management
system. Each product is identified by a unique product ID in the format
PROD-NNN (e.g., PROD-001). Real-time inventory levels are available to all
authorized agents and systems.

2. WAREHOUSE ORGANIZATION

2.1 Location Coding
Warehouse locations follow the format WH-X-NN where:
- WH: Warehouse prefix
- X: Zone letter (A=Receiving, B=Bulk Storage, C=Pick/Pack, D=Shipping, E=Quarantine)
- NN: Rack/bin number (01-99)

Example: WH-A-12 indicates Receiving Zone, Rack 12.

2.2 Storage Requirements by Category
- Mechanical parts: Standard shelving, ambient temperature, low humidity (< 60%)
- Electronic components: ESD-safe storage, temperature controlled (18-25C), humidity < 40%
- Raw materials: Per material SDS requirements, segregated by compatibility
- Packaging: Dry storage, protected from direct sunlight
- Chemicals: Ventilated storage, secondary containment, per SDS requirements

2.3 Zone Descriptions
- Zone A (Receiving): Incoming shipments staged for inspection
- Zone B (Bulk Storage): Long-term storage for high-volume items
- Zone C (Pick/Pack): Active inventory for order fulfillment
- Zone D (Shipping): Outbound orders staged for carrier pickup
- Zone E (Quarantine): Non-conforming or suspect materials pending disposition

3. RECEIVING PROCEDURES

3.1 Shipment Receipt
Step 1: Verify carrier documentation against purchase order
Step 2: Inspect packaging for visible damage (photograph if damaged)
Step 3: Count units and verify against packing slip
Step 4: Scan or enter receipt into inventory system
Step 5: Move to Zone A for quality inspection
Step 6: After inspection pass, move to designated storage location

3.2 Discrepancy Handling
- Quantity shortage: Notify procurement within 4 hours, file carrier claim if applicable
- Quantity overage: Notify procurement, hold excess in Zone A pending disposition
- Damage: Photograph, quarantine in Zone E, notify supplier and carrier
- Wrong item: Quarantine in Zone E, notify procurement for return authorization

4. INVENTORY LEVELS AND REPLENISHMENT

4.1 Reorder Point Calculation
Reorder Point = (Average Daily Usage x Lead Time in Days) + Safety Stock
Safety Stock = Z-score x Standard Deviation of Daily Usage x Square Root of Lead Time

4.2 Standard Reorder Points by Category
- Mechanical parts: 2-week supply as safety stock
- Electronic components: 3-week supply (longer lead times)
- Raw materials: 1-week supply (readily available)
- Packaging: 2-week supply
- Chemicals: 4-week supply (regulatory lead times)

4.3 Replenishment Triggers
When inventory quantity falls below the reorder point:
Step 1: System generates replenishment alert
Step 2: Procurement reviews and creates purchase order
Step 3: Order placed with preferred supplier (highest tier available)
Step 4: Expected delivery date tracked in system
Step 5: Receiving processes inbound shipment per Section 3

4.4 Critical Stock Alerts
- Yellow alert: Quantity at or below reorder point
- Orange alert: Quantity at 50% of reorder point
- Red alert: Quantity at or below safety stock level
- Stockout: Zero available quantity, escalate to procurement manager

5. INVENTORY OPERATIONS

5.1 Stock Adjustments
All inventory adjustments must be documented with:
- Product ID and current quantity
- Adjustment type: set (absolute), add (increment), or subtract (decrement)
- Reason code: receipt, shipment, damage, cycle count, return, scrap
- Authorizing personnel (adjustments > 100 units require supervisor approval)

5.2 Supported Operations
- check_inventory: Query current stock level, location, and status for a product
- update_inventory: Modify quantity using set, add, or subtract operations
  - set: Replace current quantity with specified value
  - add: Increase current quantity by specified amount
  - subtract: Decrease current quantity by specified amount (cannot go below zero)

5.3 Inventory Valuation
- Method: Weighted Average Cost
- Revaluation: Monthly, aligned with financial close
- Write-down policy: Items with no movement for 12+ months reviewed for write-down
- Obsolescence: Items with no movement for 24+ months flagged for disposition

6. CYCLE COUNTING

6.1 Count Schedule
- A-items (top 20% by value): Monthly count
- B-items (next 30% by value): Quarterly count
- C-items (remaining 50% by value): Semi-annual count
- All items: Annual full physical inventory (fiscal year end)

6.2 Count Procedures
Step 1: Generate count sheet from inventory system
Step 2: Two-person count team independently counts items
Step 3: Compare counts; if discrepancy > 2%, perform third count
Step 4: Enter final count into system
Step 5: Investigate and document any variances > 1%
Step 6: Adjust inventory with supervisor approval

6.3 Accuracy Targets
- Overall inventory accuracy: >= 99%
- A-items accuracy: >= 99.5%
- B-items accuracy: >= 98%
- C-items accuracy: >= 95%
- Location accuracy: >= 99%

7. PRODUCT CATEGORIES

7.1 Category Definitions
The inventory system organizes products into the following categories:
- mechanical_parts: Bearings, gears, shafts, fasteners, seals
- electronic_components: Circuit boards, sensors, connectors, cables
- raw_materials: Steel, aluminum, copper, plastics, composites
- packaging: Boxes, pallets, wrapping, labels, cushioning
- chemicals: Lubricants, adhesives, solvents, coatings, cleaners

7.2 Category-Specific Handling
Each category has specific handling requirements documented in the warehouse
management system. Refer to Section 2.2 for storage requirements and the
Quality Control Manual for inspection standards per category.

8. REPORTING

8.1 Standard Reports
- Daily: Stock level summary, receipts, shipments, adjustments
- Weekly: Slow-moving inventory, reorder alerts, accuracy metrics
- Monthly: Inventory valuation, turnover rates, carrying costs
- Quarterly: Obsolescence review, category performance analysis

8.2 Key Performance Indicators
- Inventory turnover ratio (target: 8-12x annually)
- Days of supply on hand (target: 30-45 days)
- Stockout rate (target: < 2%)
- Inventory accuracy (target: >= 99%)
- Carrying cost as percentage of inventory value (target: <= 25%)
